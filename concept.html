Introduction

Road accidents caused by drunk driving remain one of the leading contributors to fatalities worldwide. Despite existing laws and awareness campaigns, alcohol-impaired driving continues to endanger not only drivers but also passengers and pedestrians. Traditional methods of monitoring alcohol levels, such as checkpoints and manual testing, are reactive rather than preventive. With the rise of the Internet of Things (IoT), smart safety systems have become possible. This project develops an IoT-based alcohol detection system that monitors the driver's breath, displays the alcohol level on an LCD screen and provides a visual indicator of the driver's condition. When intoxication is detected, the system sends real-time alerts and prevents engine ignition. At the same time, a voice alert system, an annoying alarm sound and a red LED light are activated to provide immediate warnings and ensure safety.

Purpose & Objective

General Objective

To design and implement an IoT-based alcohol detection system that enhances road safety by preventing intoxicated drivers from operating vehicles

Specific Objectives

  • To develop a breath alcohol detection system using an MQ-3 alcohol sensor.
  • To integrate an LCD display that shows the detected alcohol level and provides a clear visual indicator of the driver's condition.
  • To integrate a red LED indicator that triggers simultaneously with the voice alert and annoying alarm sound when alcohol is detected.
  • To implement a safety mechanism that prevents the car from starting if alcohol levels exceed the safe limit.
  • To add a power button that allows the user to turn the system on and off easily for convenience and energy efficiency.

Concept Description

The proposed system combines alcohol sensing with multiple warning alerts and visual indicators. The MQ-3 alcohol sensor measures the driver's breath to determine the alcohol concentration, which is displayed on an LCD screen for real-time monitoring. If intoxication is detected, the system automatically prevents engine ignition while simultaneously activating a voice alert, an annoying alarm sound, and a red LED light to warn the driver. Additionally, the system includes a power button that allows the user to easily turn the device on or off, ensuring convenience, energy efficiency, and user control. This combination of alerts, visual feedback, and power management enhances safety by providing immediate and clear responses to hazardous conditions.

Significance of the Study

This project is significant because it addresses the persistent issue of drunk driving accidents. The system can:

  • Help drivers by reminding them of their fitness to drive.
  • Help society by promoting safer roads and encouraging responsible driving behavior.
  • Contribute to society by reducing accidents, injuries and fatalities caused by intoxicated driving.

Methodology

  • Alcohol Detection – The MQ-3 sensor detects the presence and concentration of alcohol from the driver's breath.
  • Processing – The microcontroller (e.g., Arduino) processes the sensor data and displays the alcohol level on the LCD screen, providing real-time feedback to the driver.
  • Decision/Alert – If the detected alcohol concentration exceeds the set threshold, the system automatically disables the vehicle's ignition and simultaneously activates a voice alert, an annoying alarm sound, and a red LED indicator to warn the driver.
  • Power Control – A dedicated power button allows the user to easily turn the system on and off, ensuring convenient operation and improved energy efficiency.

Scope & Limitation

Scope

This project focuses on developing an IoT-based alcohol detection system for vehicles. It utilizes an MQ-3 alcohol sensor to measure the driver's breath and determine alcohol concentration. If the system detects intoxication, it prevents the vehicle's ignition and activates immediate warnings through a voice alert, an annoying alarm sound, and a red LED indicator. Additionally, the system includes a power button that allows the user to easily turn the device on or off for convenience and energy efficiency. Overall, the project is designed to promote road safety by discouraging drunk driving and providing real-time feedback to the driver.

Limitations

The system is limited to detecting alcohol levels through breath analysis only and does not address other conditions such as drug use, fatigue, or medical impairments that may affect driving ability. Its effectiveness depends on the accuracy and sensitivity of the MQ-3 sensor, which may be influenced by environmental factors (e.g., smoke, strong odors, or sensor placement). Additionally, the system is intended for demonstration and prototype purposes and may require further development and testing before large-scale or commercial deployment.

Conceptual Framework

The project is based on the principles of IoT (interconnected devices exchanging data) and safety engineering (designing systems to minimize risks). The Input–Process–Output (IPO) model applies:

  • Input: Breath sample → Sensor reading
  • Process: Microcontroller analyzes data, compares to threshold
  • Output: If intoxication is detected, the system triggers a voice alert, displays the alcohol level on the LCD, activates an annoying alarm and a red LED, and engages the ignition lock to prevent the vehicle from starting. A power button is also integrated to allow the user to turn the system on or off conveniently, ensuring energy efficiency and ease of use.
